About Me

A zealous campaigner equipped with a multidisciplinary skill set that leverages data to drive strategic planning and development. I define myself on the ability to bolster anyone's workflow, acting as a conduit to channel collaboration thereby propelling projects forward.

Technically resourceful individual possessing expertise in statistical model development and scalable data storage. Specifically, skilled in scrutinizing large data sets through a combination of architecture and machine learning to yield signal within the engulfing noise.

I use this data science approach to better understand the world through the lens of an economist. With this lens I see the world as an interconnected network of systems waiting to be optimized. Harnessing this knowledge has a nearly endless reach, providing invaluable benefits such as allowing a city to automate their traffic lights, identifying the bottleneck task of a project or providing insight on how to most effectively invest resources.

Recent Projects

Predict Sales

Harnessing the power of machine learning this project offers retail stores a more efficient solution to their supply chain by identifying future sales of inventory. Allowing retailers to act preemptively and avoid negative effects from a bull whip along the supply chain.

Financial Spending

A three month service learning project for the Idaho Policy Institute(IPI); this is a project I completed for my graduate data science course at Boise State. This Project successfully makes use of Idaho's municipal government financial data by providing insightful models to augment the decision making process. This specifically correlates government expenditure and crime allowing for the optimization of all future investments.

Bioinformatics

Utilizing the B-tree data structure as a solution for memory constraints within the field of bioinformatics. This project will read and encode the entire nucleotide sequence for a strand of DNA, then allow for O(log n) search capability.
